üéØ GOAL

The Sport is about humanoid Robot Athletes.

Each Robot Athlete competes on a track where they must avoid obstacles and climb stairs.

The Robot Athlete with the highest score wins!!!

üë• TEAM ‚Äì COACH

1. Form a Team or Compete as Individual

  • 1.1 Participation: Team-based or individual
  • 1.2 Team Size: 2-3 members per team
  • 1.3 Robot Athlete Technician: Each team designates up to 1 technician

‚öôÔ∏è ROBOT ATHLETE REQUIREMENTS ‚Äì SPECIFICATIONS

ü§ñ 2. Robot Athlete Requirements

‚öôÔ∏è 2.1. GENERAL ROBOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. Any robot design allowed, if not subject to restrictions of section 2.2
  2. Dimensions: Must fit in 20cm √ó 20cm square (200mm). Max height: 50cm (500mm)
  3. Weight: Total mass at race start must be under 3kg (3000g)
  4. Humanoid Biped: Must walk on two legs and shift center of gravity to maintain balance
  5. Autonomous: All control components contained in robot. No external control systems allowed
  6. ID Number: Must display number supplied by organizers on outer casing (given after inspection)

üö´ 2.2. ROBOT RESTRICTIONS

  1. Walking Mechanics: One leg should lift off floor while other leg balances
  2. Knee Angle: Balancing leg must have knee joint angle >90 degrees while walking
  3. Feet Requirements: Robot foot = part contacting arena surface (ground)
  4. Foot Length: Max foot length < 50% of robot's extended leg length
  5. Max Foot Size: < 20cm (200mm)
  6. No Overlap: Rectangular border around left and right legs should not overlap when standing/walking
  7. Arms: Must have 2 arms. Each extended arm length ‚â§ extended leg length
  8. Head: Robot must have a head
  9. No Jamming: Jamming devices (IR LEDs to saturate opposing sensors) not allowed
  10. No Damage: Components that could break or damage playing field not permitted
  11. No Storage: Devices storing liquid, powder, gas, or other substances not allowed
  12. No Combustibles: No combustible devices of any kind
  13. No Sticky Substances: Not allowed to improve adhesion
  14. Stationary Period: Must remain stationary 5 seconds before match start. After 5 seconds, can move
  15. Activation: Can be started by remote control or button on robot

üß™ 2.3 TECHNICAL CONTROL OF ROBOT ATHLETE

  1. Initial technical inspection on game day at announced time/location
  2. Technical control before start of each phase Robot Athlete may participate in
  3. Failure to arrive on time = automatic disqualification from event
  4. Only Robot Athlete's coaches responsible for submitting robot for inspection
  5. Testing includes Robot Athlete specifications check. Non-compliance = automatic disqualification
  6. During scrutineering, coaches must demonstrate program to scrutineering committee

üèÜ COMPETITION

üìã 4. COMPETITION PROCEDURE

Coaches/team members complete technical inspection of Robot Athlete, receive unique inspection number, and run laps to qualify.

The winning Robot Athlete is the one who completes as many challenges as possible and earns the most points.

The competition consists of 2 challenges.

üßó 4.1 CHALLENGE 1: CLIMB A STAIR

üéØ 4.1.1. Objective of the Challenge

The Athlete robot must reach the top of the stair and enter the challenge area by climbing one step at a time using its legs.

üßÆ 4.1.2. Challenge Points

  • Complete Stair Climb: Robot entering challenge area after climbing = 80 points
  • Partial Climb: 10 points for each rung climbed
  • No Climb: 0 points
  • Skip Option: Robot may skip stair climb and start in challenge arena (cannot claim 80 points)

üïí 4.1.3. Tries

Robot Athlete coaches have testing time before/after efforts. Each Robot Athlete entitled to 3 official attempts where points will be counted.

ü§ñ 4.1.4. Autonomous Control

Once start signal given, robot must remain fully autonomous or will be disabled. Robot must be disconnected from any network during challenge to avoid external interference.

üîÅ 4.1.5. Restarts

  • If robot falls down ladder or gets stuck = second chance to complete challenge in 1st attempt only
  • Up to 10 minutes given to resolve problem before retrying
  • Robot will be on first step when it restarts
  • In event of restart, best effort considered for score
  • If team denies second chance or robot cannot correct in given interval = first attempt considered for score

üèÅ 4.2 CHALLENGE 2: OBSTACLE RACE

üéØ 1. Objective of the Challenge

The objective is for the bipedal Robot Athlete to complete an obstacle course while earning points.

üõ§Ô∏è 2. Challenge Course

  • Each Robot Athlete has total of 3 attempts
  • Time Limit: 5 minutes to complete per attempt
  • If robot crashes, restart allowed. Robot restarts from starting position
  • If robot doesn't complete course in 5 minutes = stop, only earned points count
  • Final classification based on total points earned by Robot Athlete
  • Teams remain in reserved space until called to playing field
  • Each team called by event official when needed in waiting area
  • After completing attempt, teams return to reserved space
  • Each team responsible for following original schedule (website + team area)
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è If called to play and don't come in 5 minutes = Robot Athlete will not compete!

‚õî 4. Interruptions

If Robot Athlete crashes for any reason, entitled to one restart. Robot must restart from starting position.

üßÆ 4.3. OBSTACLE RACE CHALLENGE RATING

üèÜ 1. Points Per Obstacle

For each obstacle the Athlete robot passes = 50 points

It should be obvious that obstacle has been passed. Robot athlete that stops for any reason in middle of obstacle will not receive points for that obstacle.
