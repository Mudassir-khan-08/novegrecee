ü§º‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è 1. DEFINITION OF WRESTLING MICRO MATCH

1.1. Definition

Match fought between two teams, each with 1+ members. Only 2 team members from each team may approach ring; only operator enters game area.

Each team competes on Dohyo (wrestling ring) with robot to specifications in Section 2. Match begins at judge's command, continues until 3 rounds completed. Judge determines winner (3-0, 2-1). After match, operator/assistant move to designated waiting area for next challenge.

ü§ñ 2. REQUIREMENTS FOR ROBOTS

2.1. General Robot Specifications

  1. Any robot design allowed (if not restricted by 2.2)
  2. üêú Max Dimensions: 5cm √ó 5cm (50mm) square - SMALLEST CATEGORY!
  3. Weight: Under 100g
  4. Expansion: May expand after match begins but must not physically separate. Must remain single centralized robot. Feet must not expand. Violation = lose match
  5. Small Parts: Parts <5g falling off won't cause match loss
  6. ‚ö° Autonomous: All components contained in robot. No external control
  7. ID Number: Must display organizer-supplied number in visible place

2.2. Robot Restrictions

  1. üõë IR Kill Switch (MANDATORY): Must have MicroStart Module Jsumo for judge operation. Not working/missing = disqualification
  2. No Jamming: IR LEDs to saturate sensors not allowed
  3. No Damage: Parts that break/damage ring not allowed. Normal pushes/bangs OK
  4. No Projectiles: Devices storing/throwing liquid, powder, gas not allowed
  5. No Flaming Devices
  6. No Throwing Devices
  7. Sticky Test: Tires/parts contacting ring must not pick up and hold A4 paper (80g/m¬≤) >2 seconds
  8. ‚ùå No Downforce: Vacuum pumps and magnets not allowed
  9. Blade Color: Blade and expansion cannot be white

üèüÔ∏è 3. REQUIREMENTS FOR DOHYO (WRESTLING RING)

3.1. Dohyo Interior

Arena's interior = playing surface surrounded by and including border line. Anywhere outside = exterior.

3.2. Dohyo Specifications

  1. Material: Circular wood, 1.25cm thick, painted black
  2. üêú Diameter: 38.5cm (385mm) - SMALLEST RING!
  3. Border Line: White circular ring, 1.25cm (12.5mm) width
  4. Height: Placed on wooden surface, 70-100cm (700-1000mm)
  5. Tolerance: ¬±5% for all dimensions
  6. Center Mark: "X" placed in center to mark robot placement area. Referee assigns quadrants

38.5cm Diameter Dohyo with white border and center X

3.3. Dohyo Exterior

Space outside outer edge of ring. Can be any color/material/shape as long as basic rule concepts not violated. This area with ring in middle called "ring area".

üïπÔ∏è 3.4. HOW TO CARRY WRESTLING MATCHES

  1. One match consists of 3 rounds
  2. If robots stuck, rules from 6.1 apply
  3. If one robot won't start: restart. Still won't start after restart = moving robot wins

3.5. Course of Competition

  • Robots divided into groups. Groups/quarters/semifinals/finals system
  • Each match: best of 3 rounds, supervised by 1 ref. Decisions final; questioning = disqualification
  • If 2 robots of same team advance to quarters/semis/finals = must play match (no forfeit/arrangement)
  • Group order random, available on website. If insufficient participants = format decided by organizing committee
  • During match: no breaks. Between matches (not rounds): modifications/repairs/reprogramming allowed if robots not removed from game area
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è If called to play and don't come in 5 minutes = robot disqualified!
  • Only operator allowed in waiting/game area
  • Max 1 min break between rounds for cleaning/configuration
  • Configuration before placing robot. Once in ring: no touching/remote changes. Attempt = point to opponent
  • Can't touch/remove robots until judge signals. Touch before signal = no objections allowed

üõ†Ô∏è 3.6. TECHNICAL CONTROL

At Beginning of Competition:

  1. Check if number exists on outer casing
  2. Photo taken of every robot (number visible)
  3. Check dimensions: 5.1√ó5.1cm (51mm) box/frame without bottom over robot
  4. Weigh on digital scale. Max 101g
  5. Check IR sensor operation for start/stop

Before Every Match:

  1. Check dimensions: 5.1√ó5.1cm box/frame
  2. Weigh on scale. Max 101g
  3. Check if number exists on casing

üö¶ 4. START, STOP, RESUME, END MATCH

4.1. Robot Placing

Upon judge's instructions, teams approach ring to place robots. Operators place simultaneously. Referees give signal. After placing, robots cannot be moved.

Robot positioned behind center X, at least one part within placement area (quadrants) defined by referee. If placement incorrect, repositioning required.

4.2. Start

Referees start every round by sending start signal with IR transmitter. As soon as robots receive signal, round starts immediately without delay.

Start made after operator in safe area. If operator leaves safe area without referee approval = team can lose point or be disqualified.

4.3. Stop, Resume

Match stops/resumes when judge announces. Referee announces when robots can be placed, when operator retreats to safe area, when robots can be taken from arena.

4.4. End

Match ends when referee announces. Teams retrieve robots from arena. After retrieval, decision final (no objections).

‚è± 5. TIME OF MATCH

5.1. Duration

One match fought for total of 3 minutes, starting/ending upon referee's command.

5.2. Extension

Extended match (if called by judge) lasts max 3 minutes

‚è≥ 6. SCORING

‚úÖ One Point Given When:

  1. Team legally forces body of opposing robot to touch space outside arena
  2. Opposing robot touched space outside arena on its own
  3. Opposing robot left ring surface completely, even if other touched outside first (after contact between robots)
  4. Opposing robot damaged without being able to continue and team representative announces it
  5. Both robots leave arena simultaneously/almost same time = robot pushed outside loses (even if pusher touches outside first)
  6. If opposing robot unable to attack (flipped over, missing wheel, cannot compete) = point awarded to other team (even if that team's robot exited arena). If team proves it's part of strategy = can normally receive point. If outcome unclear = referee calls rematch of round

‚öñÔ∏è Judge Decision Criteria:

  1. Technical merits in movement/operation
  2. Penalty points during match
  3. Attitude of players

üîÑ 6.1. MATCH STOP & REMATCH CONDITIONS

  1. Robot doesn't start: One restart. Still doesn't = moving robot wins
  2. Entangled/orbiting (10s): One restart. Repeats = robot moving more with will to fight wins
  3. Fast stuck in slow (5s): One restart. No progress/very slow after 5s = judge stops (no objections). Repeats = faster attacker wins
  4. Two fast stuck (5s): One restart. No progress/very slow after 5s = judge stops (no objections). Repeats = faster attacker wins
  5. Robot has 1pt, next no winner: Only 2 rematches. No winner in 2 rematches = 1-pt robot wins match
  6. Both move without progress/stop (5s): If one stops first = declared unwilling to fight after 5s. Opponent gets point (even if opponent stops). If unclear if progress = referee can extend time up to 30s
  7. Both touch outside simultaneously: Can't determine first = rematch

üõ†Ô∏è 6.2. REPAIRS, MODIFICATIONS, INTERRUPTIONS

From technical inspection until match ends (all rounds):

  • ‚ùå No repairs during match
  • ‚ùå No battery change/charge during match
  • ‚ùå No blade change during match
  • ‚ùå No reprogramming during match (tactics selection before fight not considered programming)
  • ‚ùå Detached blade: must continue without it until match over
  • ‚úÖ Robot must start/finish match without modification (except tactics selection) and cannot leave competition area
  • ‚úÖ Max 1 min break between rounds for cleaning/configuration
  • ‚úÖ Battery change/repairs/replacements after match ends until next match
  • If robot broken during match and can't continue = opponent wins. No repair allowed

üö´ 7. VIOLATIONS

7.1. Violations

Players performing deeds in Sections 2.2, 7.2, or 7.3 declared in violation.

7.2. Insults

  • Insulting words to opponent/judges, voice devices uttering insults, insulting words on robot body, insulting actions = violation
  • Serious Misconduct: Operator/assistant doesn't retreat to safe area or doesn't comply with referee indications
  • Serious Misconduct: Start/stop module doesn't work or missing = disqualification

7.3. Protective Gear & Safe Area

Minor violation (warning) when:

  • Enter ring during match (unless referee stops after point and contestant gets robot)
  • Demand to stop match without appropriate reasons
  • Take >60s before resuming (unless judge announces extension)
  • Does/says that disgraces fairness
  • Operator/assistant leaves waiting area without announcing to official/referee

‚û§ If team has 2 warnings = one point to opposite team OR disqualification (depends on gravity)

‚õî 8. PENALTIES

8.1. Penalties

  • Players violating rules (Sections 2.5, 7.2) lose match. Referee gives 3 points to opponent, orders violator to clear out. Violator not honored with any rights
  • Each violation (Section 7.3) accumulated. Two violations = one point to opponent
  • Violations (7.3) accumulated throughout one match
