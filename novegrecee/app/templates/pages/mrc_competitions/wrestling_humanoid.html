üéØ GOAL

The goal of the Robot Athletes is to take the opponent out of the battle on the specially designed wrestling track and be considered the winner.

There is a time limit of 4 minutes in each match (+ 1 min overtime if needed).

Wrestling, as a sport, does not require the injury or destruction of the opponent, but only displacement off the track.

‚ö†Ô∏è Robot Athletes who violate core values will be excluded and the opponent declared winner.

1. Team Formation

  • 1.1 Teams Only: Teams (not individuals) participate in the Games
  • 1.2 Team Size: Each group can consist of 2 or more people
  • 1.3 Main Trainers: Team designates 2 members as main Trainers responsible for:
    • Communication with Organizing Committee and judges
    • Technical control process

‚öôÔ∏è ROBOT ATHLETE REQUIREMENTS ‚Äì SPECIFICATIONS

2. Robot Athlete Requirements

2.1. GENERAL ROBOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. Any robot design allowed, if not subject to restrictions of section 2.2
  2. Dimensions: Must fit in 20cm √ó 20cm square (200mm). Max height: 50cm (500mm)
  3. Weight: Total mass at race start must be under 3kg (3000g)
  4. Humanoid Biped: Must walk on two legs and shift center of gravity to maintain balance when walking
  5. Autonomous: All control components contained in robot. No external control systems allowed
  6. ID Number: Must display number supplied by organizers on outer casing (found in Team Folder)

2.2. ROBOT RESTRICTIONS

  1. Walking Mechanics: One leg lifts off floor while other leg balances robot
  2. Knee Angle: Balancing leg must have knee joint angle >90 degrees while walking
  3. Feet: Robot foot = part contacting arena surface (ground)
  4. Foot Length: Max foot length < 50% of robot's extended leg length
  5. Max Foot Size: < 20cm (200mm)
  6. No Overlap: Rectangular border around left/right legs should not overlap when standing/walking
  7. Arms: Must have 2 arms. Each extended arm length ‚â§ extended leg length
  8. Head: Robot must have a head
  9. No Jamming: Jamming devices (IR LEDs to saturate opposing sensors) not allowed
  10. No Damage: Components that could damage playing field not permitted. Normal pushing/shoving allowed
  11. No Projectiles: Devices storing/throwing liquid, powder, gas, or other substances not allowed
  12. No Combustibles: No combustible devices of any kind
  13. No Sticky Substances: Not allowed to improve adhesion
  14. Stationary Period: Must remain stationary 5 seconds before match start
  15. Activation: Can be started by remote control or button on robot

üß™ 2.3 TECHNICAL CONTROL OF ROBOT ATHLETE

  1. Initial technical inspection on game day at announced time/location
  2. Technical control before start of each phase (preliminary, qualifying, final)
  3. Failure to arrive on time = automatic disqualification
  4. Only Robot Athlete's coaches responsible for submitting robot for inspection
  5. Testing includes specifications check. Non-compliance = automatic disqualification
  6. During scrutineering, coaches must demonstrate program to scrutineering committee

üèÜ COMPETITION

4. Competition Procedure

  1. Bout consists of rounds within total time of 4 minutes, unless extended by judges
  2. Extra time will not exceed 1 minute
  3. When match not won within time limit, overtime may be played (first point wins)
  4. Alternatively, winner/loser decided by judges, lot, or rematch
  5. If bots get stuck, specific rules apply (see below)
  6. If one robot doesn't start = restart. If still doesn't start after restart = moving robot wins

üìã 4.1 COURSE OF THE COMPETITION

  1. Division System: Robots divided into groups according to number of participants
  2. Competition Format: Group/Quarter/Final system for equal play rounds
  3. Judge Decisions: Must be unanimous and final. Challenging = disqualification
  4. Random Matching: Team matching by lottery after official competition start (available on website)
  5. Progression: Groups ‚Üí Quarters/Semi-finals ‚Üí Finals
  6. Alternative: If insufficient participants, pyramid system from beginning (random positions)
  7. Rescheduling Breaks: 2 breaks of 5 minutes each per team during entire competition
  8. Additional Breaks: Only for mechanical problems with referee approval (only during match)
  9. Team Areas: Teams remain in reserved area until called to playing field
  10. Waiting Area: Teams called by match referee when needed near pitch
  11. After Match: Teams return to reserved area after completion
  12. ‚ö†Ô∏è Punctuality: Each team responsible for following schedule (website + team area). DON'T BE LATE!
  13. üö´ Late Arrival: If team doesn't come to field in 5 minutes = Robot Athlete disqualified!
  14. Coaches Only: 2 Robot Athlete coaches per team. Only coaches allowed in waiting/playing area
  15. ü•Ω Safety Equipment: Coaches must wear gloves and safety glasses (checked upon approval)
  16. ‚ö†Ô∏è Missing Equipment: Lack of protective equipment = disqualification

üîç 4.2. ROBOT ATHLETE REQUIREMENTS

Each team must pass technical control stage to participate. The stages are:

  1. Dimension Check: Place 20.3cm √ó 20.3cm (203mm) box/frame without bottom on robot. Height max 50.3cm (503mm)
  2. Weight Check: Weigh robot on digital scale. Max value: 3005g
  3. Delay Check: 5 second delay will be verified
  4. Safety Equipment: Check if coaches have protective equipment
  5. ID Number: Unique number placed on outer robot casing
  6. Program Demo: Demonstration to technical committee
  7. Post-Approval: Robot not restricted after approval. Can continue testing/programming without changing dimensions (dimensions checked before race)

üéÆ 4.3. START, STOP, RESUME, END MATCH

1. Robot Placement

  • Following referee's instructions, first 2 teams approach ring to place robots
  • Operators place robots simultaneously on playing field after referee signal
  • After placement, robots may no longer move
  • After placement, coaches retreat to marked safe area
  • Position: Robot Athletes placed facing each other, 50cm distance between them
  • Referee checks correct placement. If incorrect, placement repeated

2. Beginning of Competition

  • Match starts on referee's order
  • On start command, operators start robots (must move after 5 second delay)
  • Launch after robot trainers in safe area
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è If coaches leave pitch without referee approval = team may lose point or be disqualified

3. Interruption, Continuation

Match stopped/resumed when announced by referee. Referee announces when robots can be placed, when coaches retreat to safe area, and when robots can be removed.

4. End

Match ends when referee announces it. Teams remove Robot Athletes from playing field. Once bots retrieved, decision final (no objections).

5. Race Time

  • Duration: Match played for total of 4 minutes, starting/ending at referee command
  • Extension: Extended match (if requested by referee) lasts maximum 1 minute

‚ö° KNOCKOUT

üìã SCORING DETAILS

  • Recovery Time: In all cases (except knockout), coaches given permission to place robot face down on field without penalty if it can stand up within 10-second countdown
  • Knockout Conditions:
    • Robot fails to stand up within 10 seconds
    • Robot unable to move or walk within 10 seconds
    • When knockout declared, bout immediately terminated
    • Bout awarded to opponent by 4 points
  • Points Total: All points added up for opponents for each round
  • No Progress Rule: Match may be stopped by referee if robots don't touch each other for >15 seconds with no clear will to fight

üèÜ 4.5. DETERMINATION OF WINNER

  1. Robot with most points declared winner of match
  2. In case of knockout = immediate winner
  3. In event of tie in final score = referee awards victory based on tactics, aggression, and activity
  4. If neither robot scores point = judge may decide no winner

üîÑ 4.6. MATCH REPLAY CONDITIONS

The match will be abandoned and replay started under these conditions:

  1. Robot Doesn't Start: If one robot doesn't start = restart. If same robot doesn't start on restart = point won by moving robot
  2. Bots Confused/Wandering: If robots confused/wandering around each other with no progress for 10 seconds = restart. If situation repeats:
    • Winner = robot that moves most and shows willingness to fight
    • Or = robot that moves fastest and attacks most
  3. Both Robots Stop: Both robots move making no progress, or stop (exactly same time) and stay stopped 5 seconds without touching. However:
    • If one robot stops first, after 5 seconds declared in no mood to fight
    • Opponent receives point, even if opponent also stops
    • If both moving and unclear if progress made = judge can extend time up to 30 seconds
  4. Both Touch Outer Area: If both robots touch outer playing area (white line) at approximately same time and cannot determine which touched first = replay

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: If winner cannot be declared in any above circumstances, special rule will be announced at that time.

üõ†Ô∏è 4.7. REPAIRS, MODIFICATIONS, UNFORESEEN INTERRUPTIONS

  • If robot breaks during match = referee assigns repair within 1 minute
  • Range can be extended up to 5 minutes by organizers
  • Repairs supervised by assistant referee to avoid swapping robot units with unauthorized ones
  • If robot cannot be repaired within specified range = match won by opposing robot
  • Team may continue repairs until next match under supervision of referee/organizing committee member
  • Defective parts can be replaced and batteries recharged during competition if necessary
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è If any modification made to robot after approval = robot must pass technical inspection stage again

Players performing acts in above sections declared in violation of rules.

Minor Offense (Warning):

  1. Competitor enters ring during match (unless referee stops match after point awarded to get robot)
  2. Demand to stop match without proper reasons
  3. Against referee's judgment
  4. Any action contrary to spirit of fair-play
  5. Coaches leave waiting area without notifying match referee/assistant referee
  6. ‚ö†Ô∏è 2 Warnings: Point given to opposing team OR team may be disqualified (depends on severity)
  7. Early Start: If robot starts before 5 second delay = 1 point to opponent
