üèÜ COMPETITION - GENERAL INFORMATION & RULES

Robot hockey is game based on ice hockey. Aim is to score more points than opposing team by pushing or throwing puck into goal area, making for exciting and fast-paced game. Given nature of players, ice hockey rules have been highly modified and simplified.

‚öΩ GOAL

  • Goal scored when puck crosses entire goal line
  • If puck (moving towards goal) touches defending robot whose part inside goal = defending alliance charged with goal
  • If defending robot pushed into own goal by opposing robot and puck enters goal as result (fully crossing goal line) = goal awarded

üö® PROHIBITION OF "BARRICADE" TACTICS (GOALIE CHARGING / DANCING)

‚û§ Prohibition of Obstruction via Mechanical Movement

Prohibited for any defending robot to perform rapid, repetitive, non-tactical back-and-forth movement (perpendicular to goal line) with sole purpose of blocking goal. This behavior considered anti-football and aims for passive and non-creative defense.

‚û§ Violation Criteria

Violation deemed occurred if robot:

  • (a) Repeats same small range of motion (e.g., <20cm) three (3) or more times within 5-second window
  • (b) Doesn't attempt to tactically respond to puck/opponents movement but merely "finger-taps" goal line

‚û§ Penalty

If referee determines violation:

  • Game immediately halted
  • Violating team penalized with direct free kick from point 30cm in front of center of their goal
  • If violation systematic = robot may be penalized with temporary removal (2-minute "penalty box")

üßÆ SCORE ‚Äì GROUP STAGE SCORING

No draws in robot hockey. If score tied at end of regulation time, match goes into overtime with sudden death (Golden Goal). During sudden death, additional time played as before, but match start with all robots from each team on their side of rink and outside central circle. First team to score = winner.

For Group Matches:

  • 3 points for each win
  • 0 points for each defeat
  • Alliance with most points from each Group advances to quarterfinals
  • If tie = 2 minutes extra time for "Golden Goal". If still tied after 2-minute extra time = penalty shootout procedure

For Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Short-final, Finals:

  • Alliance with most goals advances to next phase
  • If tie = 2 minutes extra time for "Golden Goal". If still tied after 2-minute extra time = penalty shootout procedure

‚öΩ PENALTY SHOOTOUT PROCEDURE

Positioning:

  • Puck placed by referee at center of rink (inside center circle and on red dot)
  • Robot Hockey Athlete executing penalty positioned inside its own goal/net (alliance chooses which Robot Hockey Athlete takes penalty kicks)

The 3 Golden Rules:

  1. Robot Hockey Athlete must first leave goal/net before touching puck
  2. Robot Hockey Athlete may only hit puck while inside center circle
  3. After hitting puck, Robot Hockey Athlete must brake before exiting circle

Execution:

  • At referee's signal, Robot Hockey Athlete starts
  • Must gain momentum, exit goal, enter center circle, and while inside circle, hit puck toward opposing goal
  • Immediately after hitting puck, must stop (brake) within circle
  • Has 20 seconds to complete entire process

When Goal Counts:

Only if puck fully crosses goal line of opposing goal under its own momentum, without Robot Hockey Athlete touching it outside circle.

Violations & Invalid Attempts:

  • If any rule violated = attempt doesn't count, considered miss (0)
  • If Robot Hockey Athlete hits puck outside circle or continues to push it = goal CANCELLED
  • If 20 seconds elapse without goal = attempt doesn't count

Determining Winner:

  • Each alliance executes 3 penalties (taken alternately)
  • Alliance with most goals wins
  • If tied = sudden-death shootout (one attempt per alliance alternately) until winner determined

‚è±Ô∏è TECHNICIAN SWITCH TIME

Between group matches, 1 minute given to each alliance. If team wishes, can change Technician. If new Technician doesn't arrive within 1 minute, previous Technician continues in next match.

‚öΩÔ∏è MATCH DURATION

  • Match lasts total of 5 (five) minutes
  • No halftime; teams retain accumulated score throughout entire match
  • Time runs continuously, no stoppages during game
  • Before each match, Robot Hockey Athlete must undergo technical inspection by referee

üéØ MATCH PREPARATION - START - RESTRICTIONS

  • Match Progression: Designed for quick movements and continuous action with few interruptions
  • Match starts with all robots from each team on their side of rink outside central circle
  • Referee throws puck approximately into center, match begins
  • During restart (goal or any occasion), robots may move anywhere within rink anytime. No exclusion zones or penalties for entering any area
  • Marking and blocking opposing robots allowed anytime. No penalties for damage caused during marking/blocking. Intentionally damaging discouraged, may lead to unsportsmanlike conduct penalty
  • To score goal, robots must move puck into opponent's goal. Goal can occur by pushing or throwing puck
  • Puck must completely cross goal line within goalposts for goal to be scored
  • Pucks thrown/flipped out of rink = penalty goal scored against team that threw/caused puck to leave. Pucks leaving due to collision or deflected by opposing robot/rink don't count as foul
  • Whenever goal scored or puck leaves rink, match time paused, robots unlocked, and if requested by team to referees, will be flipped back/substituted
  • Winner = team with most goals
  • Referee decisions final. Objection only to Headjudges. Disputing final decisions may result in team/alliance disqualification

ü§ï ROBOT HOCKEY ATHLETE INJURY

Robot Hockey Player deemed injured by referee when:

  • Some part disassembled
  • Remains stationary (lost connection to controller)

Injury Procedures:

  • Injured Robot Hockey Athlete remains out of field until repairs completed. After referee permission, returns to match
  • Returning Robot Hockey Player placed in corner of defense area (right or left, players' choice). Forbidden to place in position giving advantage in puck possession (e.g., right in front of puck). Referee can indicate which side if advantage being created
  • If Robot Hockey Athlete falls over = referee helps it get back up, continues game
  • If all Robot Hockey Athletes in alliance injured and out = time stops, restarts when even one returns
  • If Robot Hockey Athlete injured due to referee fault = timer stops, team given repair time. Time starts after return. Players and ball placed in starting position
  • If need to replace robot due to injury during match, technician informs referee. Referee gives permission without stopping match
  • If robot damaged with exposed internal parts (especially battery), referees can pause round if safety risk. Referees remove problematic robot, match restarted without injured Robot Hockey Athlete. Problematic robot cannot return without repair/replacement
  • If Robot Hockey Athlete thrown out of rink = can return only after next goal

Incapacitation Rules:

  • If all robots become incapacitated simultaneously = match terminated, winner = team with most goals
  • If all robots from one team inside arena incapacitated = match terminated, team declared loser regardless of goals. If winning team declared loser in score = fixed score 3√ó0 awarded
  • If all robots incapacitated and score tied = team whose robot last showed controlled movements declared winner. One goal awarded to break tie. If unclear which remained active last = coin toss decides winner

üéØ PENALTIES

Few penalties in Robot Hockey, purpose is to facilitate flow and prevent deliberate destruction of opposing robots.

  • Unsportsmanlike Conduct: If referee determines competitor acting in unsportsmanlike/aggressive manner = removal of one robot from team for remainder of match
  • Intentional Puck Throwing Out: If robot throws, flips, or projects puck out of rink = goal awarded to opposing team. Puck immediately placed back at center line. Pucks ejected by collision or deflected by other robots/rink don't incur penalty. Puck put into play immediately over central line
  • Non-Compliant Robots: If at scheduled match time robot doesn't comply with rules = not allowed to participate

üé≤ REFEREE ACTIONS

Referee Places Puck Back in Center:

  • When match starts
  • When goal scored
  • When referee blows whistle for "irregular marking" of opponent
  • When referee whistles "red card" on Robot Hockey Player
  • When one or more Robot Hockey Players trap puck preventing match from progressing
  • When Robot Hockey Athlete throws, flips, or projects puck out of rink

Referee Releases Puck:

When unblocks 2 or more Robot Hockey Players who trapped puck preventing match from progressing.

Robot Hockey Player Placed in Corner:

When injured Robot Hockey Player returns to game.
